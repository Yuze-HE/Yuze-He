Biography

I am a postdoctoral fellow in Computer Science Department at Carnegie Mellon University, working with Prof. Peter Steenkiste and Prof. Srinivasan Seshan. Prior to CMU, I obtained my Ph.D. degree from AIoT Lab at The Chinese University of Hong Kong supervised by Prof. Guoliang Xing. I obtained my Bachelor's degree with honors in Electrical Engineering at Zhejiang University.

My research interests lie at the intersection of wireless networking, mobile sensing, and mobile computing. My research focuses on infrastructure-assisted autonomous driving, with a vision of advancing autonomous driving with the cooperation between vehicles and intelligent roadside infrastructures. I have designed and implemented several cooperative systems on 2D/3D perception, localization, and HD mapping that have been tested in real-world scenarios. I am honored to have received the MobiCom 2023 Best Community Contribution Award, the Gold Prize at the 49th International Exhibition of Inventions Geneva, and the N2Women Young Researcher Fellowship.
